Tonya Nguyen

I am a first-year PhD student at the Berkeley School of Information, advised by Professor Niloufar Salehi. Formerly, I worked at Stanford HCI, BiD lab, and the Berkeley Center for Law and Technology. My research is in the field of human-computer interaction and social computing. In particular, I'm interested in leveraging participatory- and value-sensitive design and systems building to create alternative configurations online.

In a past life, I graduated from UC Berkeley's Interdisciplinary Studies Field program, where I curated an interdisciplinary major in Computer Science, Design Innovation, and Critical Theory, founded UC Berkeley's Beyond Design Thinking DeCal, and served as head TA of Info 213: Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction Design.

I spend a lot of time thinking about how we might democratize virtual spaces, design more fair and equitable AI, and develop technical systems that enable political participation and social wellbeing. Outside of research, my happiest moments are when I'm exploring epistemic motivations for being, moving my body (hiking the Berkeley fire trails, racing down hills on my bike, lifting weights), and acting with kindness.

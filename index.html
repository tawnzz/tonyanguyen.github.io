Tonya Nguyen
half moon bay, ca. fall 2021

I'm a second year PhD student at the Berkeley School of Information, advised by Professor Niloufar Salehi. My research is in the field of human-computer interaction, social computing, and new media. I'm passionate about democratizing algorithmic systems through the engagement of marginalized communities, operationalizing core values embedded in technology, and developing sociotechnical systems that enable civic participation and radical, collective care.

In the past, I researched task design and team viability at Stanford HCI, built chatbots to understand how listening and talking to an AI can lead to more instances of self-disclosure, and drove the design direction of UC Berkeley's largest non-partisan political magazine.

My research career began at UC Berkeley, where I curated an interdisciplinary major in Computer Science, Design, and Critical Theory (2020). For my undergraduate thesis, I wrote about how online affordances and algorithmic curation within ethnic-based, virtual counterpublics enable the emergence of diasporic identity work. I am lucky to have been involved with the Berkeley Basic Needs Center, Innovative Design, ASUC OCTO, and Barbell at Berkeley.
